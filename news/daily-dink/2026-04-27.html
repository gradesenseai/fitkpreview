FITK Daily Dink - April 27, 2026

Some of the top stories moving in pro pickleball today, from the UPA-A's first official 71-page pro rulebook taking effect at MLP Dallas to the bubble races at Veolia Atlanta as the last 2,000-point stop before PPA Finals.

  • Pro pickleball has a new rulebook as the UPA-A drops a 71-page playbook for the PPA Tour and MLP

    RulesUPA-APPA TourMajor League PickleballPaddle Testing

    The United Pickleball Association of America released its first official 71-page rulebook for professional pickleball, with new standards covering serving, paddle challenges, line calls, on-court behavior, and a Go-No-Go paddle testing machine that takes effect May 22, 2026 at the MLP season opener in Dallas. The release follows the UPA-A's IRS approval as a not-for-profit independent organization and is positioned to standardize officiating across the PPA Tour and Major League Pickleball domestically and internationally.

  • Ranking points take center stage at Veolia Atlanta Pickleball Championships

    PPA TourVeolia Atlanta ChampionshipsBen JohnsGabe TardioPPA Finals

    Pickleball.com previewed the Veolia Atlanta Pickleball Championships, the final 2,000-point Slam of the 2025-2026 PPA Tour season and the last chance for pros to lock in qualifying spots for the PPA Finals May 4-10 in San Clemente. The breakdown highlights bubble races across all five divisions, with Ben Johns and Gabe Tardio entering as undefeated men's doubles favorites and players like Augie Ge, Riley Newman, and Liz Truluck still chasing finals berths.
